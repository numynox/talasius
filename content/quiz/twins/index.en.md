Standing side by side are the _Duomo Vecchio_ (11th century), one of the rare circular Romanesque churches in Italy, and the _Duomo Nuovo_, whose construction began in 1604 but was not completed until the 19th century. Because the works took so long, the old cathedral was never torn down – and so both remain standing.
